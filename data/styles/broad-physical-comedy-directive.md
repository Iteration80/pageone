## Scene Construction
Open scenes with a simple comic problem that can be understood visually: hide the mess, deliver the cake, keep the lie alive, sneak past security, survive the dinner, fix the machine, or appear normal while everything goes wrong. Establish the rules of the gag before escalating it.

Build scenes in clear ladders. Each beat should make the physical situation worse, more public, more fragile, or more absurd. End scenes with a strong button: collapse, exposure, reversal, accidental success, or the next problem created by solving this one.

## Action Lines
Write physical action as readable choreography. Track bodies, props, entrances, exits, distances, timing, and cause-effect. Make every fall, spill, collision, near miss, or disguise mistake mechanically clear before the punchline hits.

Use short paragraphs and active verbs. Capitalize key sound effects or prop impacts when needed. Keep the camera on what the audience must see to understand the gag.

## Dialogue
Let dialogue serve the physical bit. Characters deny what is visibly happening, over-explain a failing plan, whisper instructions at the worst possible time, or try to maintain dignity while their body betrays them.

Use repetition with escalation. A phrase, excuse, or instruction should become funnier each time the situation deteriorates.

## Tone
Keep the comedy generous and energetic. Humiliation can be funny, but cruelty should not be the engine. Let characters be competent in one area and disastrously overmatched in another.

## Signature Moves
**Visible Plan Failure:** The audience understands the plan and watches every part of it break.

**Prop Domino:** One small object triggers a chain of increasingly public consequences.

**Dignity Under Attack:** A character tries to remain composed while physics, timing, or costume destroys the pose.

## Avoid
Do not write vague chaos. Do not depend on characters being stupid when panic, pride, or bad timing can motivate the mistake. Do not skip setup. Do not let verbal jokes replace the physical progression.
