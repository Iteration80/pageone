## Scene Construction
Open scenes with ritual, omen, landscape, vow, threshold, prophecy, battle consequence, or ancient rule made immediate. Give every scene a present objective and a deeper mythic pressure: lineage, oath, exile, sacrifice, temptation, inheritance, or forbidden knowledge.

Build scenes through choices that echo beyond the moment. A private mercy, broken promise, accepted burden, or refused call should feel like it can alter kingdoms, families, gods, or generations. End scenes on a fateful image, irreversible decision, revealed cost, or crossing into a place that changes the rules.

## Action Lines
Write with elevated clarity. Use strong images, concrete terrain, artifacts, weather, banners, ruins, fires, beasts, armor, songs, and thresholds. Keep prose cinematic, not ornate. Let worldbuilding appear through action and consequence rather than explanation.

Name magical or sacred elements with restraint. A named sword, gate, oath, star, river, or crown should matter to behavior, not just flavor.

## Dialogue
Give dialogue weight without stiffness. Characters may speak formally when ritual or rank demands it, but private exchanges should retain human urgency. Let vows, bargains, curses, and blessings carry plot force.

Use subtext around duty. Characters argue about strategy, honor, law, or prophecy while really fighting fear, loyalty, grief, or desire.

## Tone
Maintain awe and consequence. Wonder should sit beside danger. Let beauty have a cost. Treat the fantastic as real to the characters and governed by rules they respect or fear.

## Signature Moves
**Threshold Moment:** A character crosses a visible boundary and cannot return unchanged.

**Small Mercy, Vast Cost:** A humane choice disrupts a larger order.

**Object with History:** A prop carries memory, debt, or prophecy into the scene.

## Avoid
Do not drown scenes in lore. Do not use archaic language so heavily that emotion disappears. Do not make magic solve problems without cost. Do not let spectacle replace the character's moral choice.
