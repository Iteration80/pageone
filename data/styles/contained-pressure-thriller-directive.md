## Scene Construction
Open scenes with a constraint already active: a locked door, a dying battery, a countdown, a narrowing search radius, a blocked exit, a body hidden nearby, or a lie about to be tested. Make the location matter. Every scene should use the room, vehicle, building, bunker, office, ship, train, or house as both geography and antagonist.

Build scenes by tightening options. Each beat should remove a path, expose a cost, or force a character to choose between two bad outcomes. End scenes on a new limitation: less time, less space, fewer allies, worse information, or a compromised plan.

## Action Lines
Write lean, tactical action. Track doors, windows, distances, sounds, light sources, weapons, tools, and sightlines. Keep descriptions practical and spatially clear. Use short paragraphs to increase pressure and make movement readable.

Let silence and small sounds carry dread. A ventilation rattle, a shoe on glass, a phone vibration, a key turning, or a breath held too long can be as important as violence.

## Dialogue
Keep dialogue economical. Characters speak because they need information, compliance, distraction, reassurance, or time. Use clipped sentences under pressure. Let subtext emerge through what characters refuse to say while survival demands honesty.

Avoid speeches unless the character is using the speech tactically: stalling, manipulating, confessing to gain trust, or pushing someone into action.

## Tone
Maintain compression. The story should feel like the walls are moving inward. Let fear be practical before it is poetic. Hope should arrive as a plan, not a mood.

## Signature Moves
**Constraint Flip:** The thing that protected the character becomes the thing trapping them.

**Tool Reversal:** A mundane object is repurposed under pressure, then becomes a liability.

**Quiet Countdown:** Time pressure appears through physical details rather than repeated reminders.

## Avoid
Do not cut away from the main pressure too often. Do not add new locations just for variety. Do not let characters ignore obvious exits or tools. Do not slow the engine with abstract dread when a concrete obstacle can do the work.
