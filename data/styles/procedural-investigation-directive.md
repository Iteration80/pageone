## Scene Construction
Open scenes inside process: a briefing, canvass, lab result, warrant request, field interview, case board update, surveillance shift, autopsy note, data search, or chain-of-command decision. Give every scene a job in the investigation and a human complication that makes the job harder.

Build scenes through evidence and procedure. Characters should earn progress by asking better questions, connecting records, noticing inconsistencies, or making disciplined choices under institutional limits. End scenes on actionable information: a name, location, contradiction, deadline, jurisdiction problem, or ethical line.

## Action Lines
Write with practical specificity. Track files, time stamps, radio calls, badges, gloves, reports, maps, phones, cameras, entry logs, and physical evidence. Keep geography clear and workspaces credible. Let competence be cinematic through precision, not jargon overload.

Use process as pacing. A printer warming up, a database search stalling, a warrant waiting on a judge, or a surveillance team losing sight of a target can carry tension.

## Dialogue
Keep dialogue task-driven. Characters speak to clarify facts, challenge assumptions, assign work, manage risk, or push against bureaucracy. Let personal tension surface through professional disagreement.

Use technical language sparingly and define it through context. The audience should understand what a clue means and why it changes the next move.

## Tone
Respect competence. The system can be flawed, slow, political, or morally compromised, but the investigators should think clearly. Maintain pressure through deadlines, public stakes, and the cost of getting it wrong.

## Signature Moves
**Evidence Chain:** A tiny documented detail leads logically to the next scene.

**Professional Mask Crack:** A character reveals emotion only after the job forces it out.

**Procedure vs. Instinct:** A correct hunch must still survive process before action.

## Avoid
Do not fake complexity with empty acronyms. Do not let investigators skip obvious steps. Do not solve cases through lucky guesses. Do not make every authority figure obstructive; selective friction is stronger.
