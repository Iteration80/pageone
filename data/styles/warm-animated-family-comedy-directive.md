## Scene Construction
Build scenes around clear emotional misunderstandings, visible wishes, and small physical problems that escalate into comic set pieces. Open with behavior, not explanation. Let characters reveal who they are through how they move through a room, handle a prop, dodge embarrassment, or misunderstand a simple instruction.

Each scene should carry a child-readable surface goal and an adult-readable emotional undercurrent. End scenes on a clean emotional or comic turn: a failed plan, a surprising kindness, a new worry, or a joke that exposes character.

## Action Lines
Write action with bright physical specificity. Use objects, space, and timing to make emotion visible. Keep paragraphs short and animated, but avoid empty bounce. Every movement should reveal desire, panic, pride, curiosity, fear, or affection.

Let the world feel tactile and playable. Doors stick. Backpacks overflow. Chairs spin. Snacks spill. Small visual details can carry jokes, but the prose should remain simple and direct.

## Dialogue
Keep dialogue accessible, rhythmic, and emotionally legible. Adults may deflect, over-explain, or pretend control. Kids should cut through complexity with blunt literal logic, questions, and sudden honesty.

Let jokes come from character mismatch rather than sarcasm alone. Use interruptions, half-finished thoughts, and quick reversals. When the emotional truth arrives, let it be plain.

## Tone
Protect sincerity. Humor can be fast, silly, and physically big, but it should never mock the characters for caring. Treat family wounds, loneliness, fear, and belonging with full seriousness.

## Signature Moves
**Literal Logic:** Let a child or innocent character puncture adult complexity with one practical observation.

**Comic Object Escalation:** A small prop problem grows into a visual chain reaction.

**Soft Landing:** After chaos, give the scene one clear emotional image.

## Avoid
Do not write mean-spirited jokes. Do not overuse pop-culture references. Do not make characters self-aware about genre. Do not solve emotion with speeches when a concrete gesture can land it.
