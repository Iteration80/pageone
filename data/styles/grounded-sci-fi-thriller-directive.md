## Scene Construction
Open with a concrete anomaly, operational problem, or human decision under technical pressure. Let each scene advance both the mystery and the cost of understanding it. Build tension through procedure, observation, tests, failed assumptions, and ethical pressure.

End scenes when new information changes the rules or when a character chooses a morally loaded next step. Keep the science legible through behavior and consequence rather than explanation dumps.

## Action Lines
Write with clean observational precision. Focus on instruments, readings, containment lines, environmental changes, body language under stress, and the physical reality of technology. Use short paragraphs and exact nouns.

Avoid awe language. Let the image be strange without the prose announcing wonder. When scale is large, ground it in a small measurable detail: a pressure change, a hairline crack, a delayed signal, a failing battery.

## Dialogue
Keep dialogue practical and pressured. Characters speak to solve problems, buy time, challenge assumptions, or hide fear. Use technical vocabulary only when it clarifies a decision.

Let emotion leak through omissions, repeated checks, clipped answers, and small corrections. Avoid monologues about the theme. Let ethics emerge from what people choose when information is incomplete.

## Tone
Maintain controlled dread and human intimacy. The world should feel plausible, tactile, and governed by cause and effect. Mystery is strongest when characters behave intelligently and still remain outmatched.

## Signature Moves
**Measurement Under Stress:** Use readings, limits, and tolerances to create suspense.

**Small Detail, Large Threat:** A tiny physical clue reveals a catastrophic implication.

**Ethical Procedure:** A protocol forces a character to choose between rule and person.

## Avoid
Do not use vague technobabble. Do not pause for lore lectures. Do not make experts foolish for convenience. Do not describe cosmic stakes with abstract grandeur when a concrete failure point is available.
