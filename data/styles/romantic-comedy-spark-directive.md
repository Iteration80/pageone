## Scene Construction
Open scenes on social pressure already in motion: a bad date, an awkward work task, a family expectation, a public mistake, or a private feeling about to leak. Give each scene a visible comic objective and a hidden emotional objective. Let the surface want be practical while the deeper want is connection, safety, pride, forgiveness, or being truly seen.

Build scenes through attraction and resistance. Characters should be drawn toward each other and pushed apart by timing, ego, fear, obligation, or misread signals. End scenes on a shift in intimacy: a new spark, a wound exposed, a lie maintained, a gesture misunderstood, or a joke that lands too close to truth.

## Action Lines
Write action with lightness and precision. Use small behavioral tells: a glance held too long, a hand hovering near a phone, a smile recovered half a beat late, an outfit adjusted for confidence. Keep paragraphs short and readable. Let physical business reveal what characters are trying not to say.

Use public spaces as emotional pressure cookers. Restaurants, elevators, offices, weddings, sidewalks, and kitchens should create interruptions, witnesses, and comic obstacles.

## Dialogue
Write dialogue as verbal fencing. Characters tease, deflect, challenge, and test each other, but the jokes should hide sincere need. Let banter accelerate when feelings get dangerous. Use interruptions, callbacks, and precise reversals.

Keep exposition inside flirtation, argument, or embarrassment. Let characters reveal backstory because they are trying to win, escape, impress, or protect themselves.

## Tone
Keep the world emotionally generous. Conflict can sting, but the style believes people are capable of repair. Balance wit with real vulnerability. Let sincerity arrive before the scene fully admits it.

## Signature Moves
**The Joke That Means It:** A comic line lands as a confession the speaker can pretend was casual.

**Public Private Moment:** Two people find intimacy in a crowded or chaotic place.

**Callback as Courtship:** A repeated phrase, prop, or tiny observation becomes proof that someone was paying attention.

## Avoid
Do not make characters cruel for easy laughs. Do not let banter become detachable quipping. Do not solve conflict with one speech if behavior has not changed. Do not treat romance as inevitable; earn attraction through choice, attention, and risk.
