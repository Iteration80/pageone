## Scene Construction
Open scenes on measurable struggle: a missed shot, failed drill, bad time, empty gym, hostile crowd, injury, humiliating loss, team argument, or coach's impossible standard. Give each scene both a sports goal and an emotional skill the character has not yet mastered.

Build scenes through training, friction, setback, adjustment, and earned improvement. Let physical progress reveal inner change. End scenes on a new challenge, small victory, exposed weakness, team fracture, or choice to keep going when quitting would be easier.

## Action Lines
Write athletic action with clear mechanics. Track bodies, fatigue, rhythm, field position, court spacing, weather, equipment, breath, sweat, and pain. Make the sport readable even to viewers who do not know the rules by focusing on objective, obstacle, and consequence.

Use repetition to show discipline. A drill repeated badly, then differently, then under pressure can carry story.

## Dialogue
Keep dialogue direct and pressure-tested. Coaches challenge, teammates needle, families worry, rivals expose insecurity, and athletes deflect vulnerability through competition. Let speeches be earned by prior failure.

Use sports language as emotional metaphor only when grounded in action. A character should learn the lesson by doing, not by hearing a slogan.

## Tone
Make effort honorable. The style should believe in work without pretending work guarantees victory. Balance grit with uplift. Let losses matter and victories cost something.

## Signature Moves
**Training Echo:** A repeated practice beat pays off under game pressure in a changed emotional context.

**Team Before Talent:** The protagonist advances by trusting someone else, not by becoming magically better alone.

**Body Tells Truth:** Injury, exhaustion, or hesitation reveals what the character is hiding.

## Avoid
Do not reduce opponents to villains unless the story demands it. Do not skip the mechanics of improvement. Do not let motivational speeches replace action. Do not make the final score the only emotional outcome.
